What is the best country to live in as a woman?


As a woman, it is important to feel safe and secure in the country you live in. With this in mind, it is important to consider the best countries to live in as a woman. To determine the best countries to live in as a woman, the World Economic Forum has created the Global Gender Gap Index, which measures gender equality in four areas: economic participation and opportunity, educational attainment, health and survival, and political empowerment.

Top Performers on the Global Gender Gap Index

The top four performers on the index are all Nordic countries—Norway, Finland, Iceland, and Denmark—while Sweden, the other Nordic country, ranks seventh. All five countries have closed more than 80% of their gender gaps, and have achieved parity in educational attainment and health and survival.

Norway

Norway ranks first on the Global Gender Gap Index, with a score of 0.84. Norway has achieved parity in educational attainment, health and survival, and political empowerment, and has closed 82.7% of its economic gender gap. Norway has achieved gender equality in the workplace by introducing quotas for female board members and providing generous parental leave.
