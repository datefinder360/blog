How do you keep a conversation going without being awkward?


Having a conversation with someone can be an intimidating experience, especially if you don't know the person very well. It can be difficult to know how to keep the conversation going without being awkward. Fortunately, there are a few simple tips and tricks that can help you keep a conversation going without being awkward.

What is Conversation?

Conversation is a two-way exchange of information and ideas between two or more people. It is a form of communication that allows people to connect with each other and build relationships. In order to have a successful conversation, it is important to be able to keep the conversation going without being awkward.

Be Brave, Worry Less

Even if it's uncomfortable, be brave and just do it, Sandstrom says. “It's easy to get caught up in worrying about how you sound or how the other person will react, but if you just take a deep breath and jump in, you'll find that the conversation will flow more naturally.”

Be Curious

Ask questions. One of the best ways to keep a conversation going is to ask questions. This will show the other person that you are interested in what they have to say and will help keep the conversation going.

Don't Be Afraid to Go Off-Script

Don't be afraid to go off-script. If the conversation starts to feel stale, don't be afraid to change the topic or ask a different question. This will help keep the conversation interesting and prevent it from becoming awkward.

Give Someone a Compliment

Giving someone a compliment is a great way to keep a conversation going. It will show the other person that you are interested in them and will make them feel more comfortable.

Talk About Something You Both Have in Common

Finding something that you both have in common is a great way to keep a conversation going. It will give you something to talk about and will help you both connect on a deeper level.

Have More Conversations With People You Don't Know

Having conversations with people you don't know can be intimidating, but it is also a great way to practice conversation skills. The more conversations you have, the more comfortable you will become with talking to new people.

Don't Let the Awkward Moments Trip You Up

It is inevitable that there will be awkward moments in any conversation. Don't let these moments trip you up. Instead, take a deep breath and move on.

Having a conversation with someone can be intimidating, but it doesn't have to be awkward. By following these simple tips, you can keep a conversation going without being awkward. Be brave, be curious, don't be afraid to go off-script, give someone a compliment, talk about something you both have in common, have more conversations with people you don't know, and don't let the awkward moments trip you up. With these tips, you can have a successful conversation without feeling awkward.
