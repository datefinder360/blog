How Hinge Decides Who Sees You: An Overview

When it comes to online dating, it's not just about finding someone you like, but also someone who is likely to like you back. That's why Hinge, a popular dating app, has developed a sophisticated algorithm to pair users who are likely to mutually like one another. Here's how Hinge decides who sees you.

Data Collection

Hinge collects data from its users to help it make better matches. This includes information such as who you like, who you send comments to, and who you are having conversations with. Over time, Hinge uses this data to create a better understanding of who you are and what type of person you are likely to be compatible with.

Algorithm

Hinge uses an algorithm to determine who you are likely to be compatible with. This algorithm takes into account factors such as age, location, interests, and lifestyle. It also takes into account the data that Hinge has collected about you, such as who you like, who you send comments to, and who you are having conversations with.

Matchmaking

Once the algorithm has determined who you are likely to be compatible with, Hinge will then match you with those users. This means that when you open the app, you will be presented with a list of potential matches who are likely to be compatible with you.
