Understanding the Cost of Living in Alaska

Before diving into what a livable salary in Alaska is, it’s important to understand the cost of living in the state. According to the Economic Policy Institute, the cost of living in Alaska is 18.2% higher than the national average. This means that, on average, it costs 18.2% more to live in Alaska than it does in the rest of the United States.

What is a Livable Salary in Alaska?

Now that you understand the cost of living in Alaska, let’s discuss what a livable salary is in the state. According to the Economic Policy Institute, a livable salary in Alaska is $48,000 per year for a single adult and $97,000 per year for a family of four. This is significantly higher than the national median income of $61,937 for a single adult and $98,711 for a family of four.
