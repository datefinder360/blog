Why Don't I Want to Be Intimate with My Husband?


It is not uncommon for married couples to experience a decrease in their desire for physical intimacy. In fact, it is estimated that around 20-30% of married couples are affected by a lack of sexual desire. If you are one of those couples, it is important to understand why you may not be feeling the same level of intimacy with your partner.

Common Causes of Low Sex Drive

Low sex drive can be a result of many things, including menopause, libido reducing medication, pain during intercourse, and decreased libido due to age. However, the most common reason for losing interest in sex is a troubled relationship. Even if you think that everything is fine, it is possible that some issues are being swept under the rug.

Understanding the Reasons

If you are not feeling the same level of intimacy with your husband, it is important to take the time to understand why. It could be due to a lack of communication, unresolved issues, or a feeling of disconnection. It is also possible that you are feeling overwhelmed with other aspects of your life, such as work or parenting.

Getting to the Root of the Problem

If you are struggling to understand why you don't want to be intimate with your husband, it is important to take the time to get to the root of the problem. Talk to your partner about your feelings and try to identify any underlying issues that may be causing a rift in your relationship.

Seeking Professional Help

If you are still struggling to identify the cause of your lack of intimacy, it may be beneficial to seek professional help. A therapist or counselor can help you and your partner work through any issues that may be preventing you from feeling close to one another.

Reigniting the Spark

Once you have identified the root of the problem, it is important to take steps to reignite the spark in your relationship. This can include spending more quality time together, engaging in activities that you both enjoy, and taking the time to express your love and appreciation for one another.
