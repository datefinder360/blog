5 Things You Should Never Put on a Dating Profile

In the world of online dating, your profile is your first impression. It's important to make sure you don't make any mistakes that could turn off potential matches. Here are five things you should never put on a dating profile.

What You're Not Looking For

Including criteria for who shouldn't swipe left or message you is just a real Debbie Downer move. It's better to focus on the qualities you are looking for in a partner, rather than the qualities you don't want.

Anything Too Generic

Using generic phrases like "I'm looking for someone who loves to laugh" or "I'm looking for someone who is kind and understanding" can make your profile seem bland and unoriginal. Try to be more specific about the qualities you're looking for in a partner.

Sarcasm About Being on 'The Apps'

Sarcasm is often lost in the world of online dating. It's best to avoid making jokes about being on the apps, as it can come off as negative and unappealing.

Lies (or Even Exaggerations)

It's important to be honest in your profile. Lying or exaggerating about your interests, hobbies, or even your age can be a major turn-off for potential matches.

No Information

Leaving your profile blank or with minimal information is a surefire way to ensure no one will swipe right on you. Make sure to include some interesting facts about yourself and what you're looking for in a partner.

The Bottom Line

Creating a successful dating profile is all about being honest and avoiding common mistakes. Make sure to avoid including what you're not looking for, anything too generic, sarcasm about being on the apps, lies or exaggerations, and no information. With a little bit of effort, you can create a profile that will help you find the perfect match.
