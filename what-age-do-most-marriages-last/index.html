What age do most marriages last?


Marriage is a lifelong commitment that many couples strive to make last. While there is no single answer to the question of what age do most marriages last, there are some trends that can help us understand the average age of divorce.

The Average Age of Divorce

The average age for people going through a divorce for the first time is 30 years old. According to a recent report, more than half, or 60%, of divorces involve spouses who are between the ages of 25 and 39. This means that the majority of divorces occur during the peak of a couple's life, when they are likely to be in their prime earning years and have children.

Divorce Rates for People Over 50

While 30 is the average age, the divorce rate for people over 50 has doubled since 1990. This is due to a variety of factors, including the fact that people are living longer and healthier lives, making it easier for them to stay in relationships that may no longer be fulfilling. Additionally, more people are getting married later in life, meaning that they may not have the same level of commitment to the marriage as those who married younger.

Factors That Impact Marriage Duration

The age at which a couple gets married is not the only factor that can impact the longevity of a marriage. Other factors, such as financial stability, communication, and shared values, can also play a role in determining how long a marriage will last. Additionally, couples who have a strong support system of family and friends are more likely to stay together than those who don't.

Conclusion

While there is no single answer to the question of what age do most marriages last, there are some trends that can help us understand the average age of divorce.
