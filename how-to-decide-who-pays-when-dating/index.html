How to Decide Who Pays When Dating

When it comes to dating, it can be difficult to know who should pay for the bill. It is important to consider both parties' feelings and preferences to ensure that the date goes smoothly. This article will provide some tips on who should pay when dating.

1. Whoever Asks for the Date Should Pay

The traditional rule of thumb is that whoever asks for the date should pay the bill. This is a polite way to show respect for the other person and to make sure that the date is enjoyable for both parties.

2. If Your Date Set Up the Plans, They Should Pay

On the flip side, if your date set up your dinner plans, they'll probably be the one to pay for everything. This is a nice gesture and shows that they are willing to put in the effort to make the date a success.

3. Offer to Pay

Even if you don't plan on paying for the bill, it's still polite to reach for your wallet whenever the check comes. This gesture shows that you are willing to pay for the date and that you are respectful of the other person's time and money.

4. Split the Bill

If both parties are comfortable with it, splitting the bill is also an option. This is a great way to show that you are both invested in the date and that you are both willing to contribute to the cost.
